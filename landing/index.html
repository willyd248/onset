Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know before you hit play.

Any controller that exposes a standard MIDI interface works with Onset — that covers nearly every modern DJ controller including Pioneer DDJ series, Hercules Inpulse series, Numark, and Roland. We officially test on the Hercules Inpulse 200 MK2. If your controller supports WebMIDI (works in Chrome/Edge), it works with Onset.

Yes, genuinely free. No credit card, no 7-day trial, no feature-gating after a week. The core 30 lessons, real-time scoring, and progress tracking are permanently free. We'll add a paid Pro tier for advanced lessons and analytics, but the fundamentals stay free. We'd rather have you actually learning than chasing a conversion.

Nothing. Onset runs entirely in your browser using Web Audio API and WebMIDI API. Just open Chrome or Edge, plug in your controller, and go. No downloads, no drivers (on most systems), no accounts required to start. We do ask for your email to save progress across sessions.

Chrome and Edge on desktop (1200px+ screen recommended). Firefox works for most features but has limited WebMIDI support. Safari does not support WebMIDI and is not supported. Mobile browsers are not yet supported — Onset is designed for a full desktop experience with an actual controller.

Any audio files you own — MP3, WAV, OGG, FLAC. Drag them onto a deck and Onset auto-detects the BPM and generates a beat grid. We don't include licensed tracks, so you bring your own music. Most beginners practice with tracks they already know well, which actually helps you focus on the technique.

Yes, though the experience is limited. You can use your mouse and keyboard for basic lesson demonstrations, but the real-time scoring requires a MIDI controller to track your physical gestures. If you're serious about learning, a ~$100 Hercules Inpulse 200 MK2 is the recommended starting point. We also have a phone-as-controller feature in development.
